Summary of the two novels

"The Graveyard Book" by Neil Gaiman tells the story of Bod, a young boy who seeks refuge in a graveyard after his family is murdered. Raised by supernatural beings, Bod learns their ways and abilities while navigating the boundary between the living and the dead. As he grows older, Bod's curiosity about the outside world grows, leading to encounters with friends and enemies. With the threat of his family's killer still present, Bod must confront his fears and protect his loved ones. Gaiman's enchanting tale explores themes of family, friendship, and self-discovery in a captivating blend of fantasy and adventure.

"A Wrinkle in Time" by Madeleine L'Engle is a beloved science fiction novel that follows the adventures of Meg Murry, her brother Charles Wallace, and their friend Calvin O'Keefe. When their father mysteriously disappears while working on a secret government project, the three children embark on a journey through space and time to rescue him. Guided by three celestial beings, Mrs. Whatsit, Mrs. Who, and Mrs. Which, they travel to other planets in search of their father and confront the evil force known as the Dark Thing. Meg discovers her own inner strength as they face mind-bending challenges and discover the power of love and acceptance.

Brief introduction of the
writers

Neil Gaiman

Neil Gaiman is a celebrated British author known for his imaginative contributions to fantasy, science fiction, and horror. With a captivating storytelling style, he has crafted acclaimed works such as "American Gods," "Coraline," and "The Sandman" series, earning him numerous prestigious awards and a devoted global following.

Madeleine L'Engle

Madeleine L'Engle was an esteemed American author known for her remarkable contributions to children's literature and science fiction. With works like "A Wrinkle in Time" and its sequels, L'Engle's writing blended elements of fantasy, adventure, and deep philosophical themes, captivating readers of all ages. Her legacy continues to inspire generations of readers worldwide.
